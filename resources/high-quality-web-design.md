# High-Quality Web Design

## Purpose

This reference defines what "high-quality" should mean when evaluating visual-direction work in this repository.

Use it as supporting guidance for step `06-visual-direction`, not as a replacement for the workflow contract.

## Core Principles

High-quality web design is usually marked by control, not by more decoration.

- Clear hierarchy: users should understand what matters first, second, and third within a few seconds.
- Strong scale discipline: a few intentional size levels work better than many competing text sizes.
- Useful whitespace: spacing should create rhythm, grouping, and calm, not emptiness for its own sake.
- One dominant compositional move: strong pages usually have a clear focal structure instead of equal-weight blocks all the way down.
- Purposeful imagery: images should add proof, atmosphere, or orientation, not filler.
- Restrained surfaces: borders, shadows, radius, and color accents should support structure instead of becoming the structure.
- Readability and trust: typography, contrast, and mobile behavior affect perceived professionalism as much as visual style.

## What Premium Usually Looks Like

For this workflow, "professional and elegant" should usually mean:

- clear visual priority
- measured typography
- confident whitespace
- strong image cropping and placement
- fewer but better surface treatments
- obvious scanning paths
- less repeated card framing

It should not depend on:

- stacking many rounded cards
- equal-height panels in every section
- decorative icons everywhere
- oversized gradients or shadows doing all the work
- generic stock imagery with no clear role
- too many visual accents competing for attention

## Research-Backed Quality Signals

### 1. Hierarchy beats density

If everything is similarly sized, similarly boxed, or similarly emphasized, the design feels template-like. Good layouts create emphasis through contrast in size, position, spacing, and image dominance.

### 2. Whitespace should clarify structure

Whitespace is useful when it separates priorities, creates section rhythm, and makes scanning easier. It becomes wasteful when it only inflates the layout without adding clarity.

### 3. Typography carries credibility

Professional-looking websites typically use a small number of type sizes and weights consistently. The type scale should feel intentional and easy to scan on desktop and mobile.

### 4. Imagery must earn its space

High-quality sites usually use fewer, stronger images with better crops and more deliberate placement. Small filler images often make layouts feel busy and lower trust.

### 5. Decoration should serve meaning

Borders, dividers, background textures, shadows, and icons should clarify grouping or emphasis. If they exist only to make the page look "designed", they usually weaken the result.

### 6. Premium does not mean fragile

A refined design still needs to work responsively, preserve readability, and feel trustworthy on mobile. Visual direction that only works at one hero viewport is not high quality.

## Step 06 Evaluation Checklist

### Composition

- Is there one obvious focal move, or is the page just stacked sections?
- Does the layout create rhythm through contrast, not just spacing?
- Do sections feel intentionally different in weight, or interchangeable?

### Typography

- Are the heading sizes clearly prioritized?
- Does the type feel controlled rather than loud?
- Is the body text easy to scan and believable for a real website?

### Whitespace

- Does spacing improve grouping and legibility?
- Does the page feel calm rather than sparse or crowded?
- Is the spacing rhythm consistent?

### Imagery

- Do images feel chosen, cropped, and placed with intent?
- Is there at least one image moment strong enough to anchor the concept?
- Are images adding proof or atmosphere instead of filler?

### Surfaces and details

- Are cards, borders, and shadows supporting structure rather than dominating it?
- Are corners, strokes, and elevation consistent?
- Do icons feel necessary and stylistically coherent?

### Generic-risk check

- If the copy changed, would this still feel distinctive?
- If the cards disappeared, would the composition still hold up?
- Does it look like a real brand direction, or a polished template?
