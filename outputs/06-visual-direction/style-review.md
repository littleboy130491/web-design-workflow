# Style Review

## How To Respond

Use short comments tied to one concept at a time. Good examples:

- `A heading too loud`
- `A spacing slightly too tight`
- `B feels too soft for construction`
- `B serif direction works, but imagery should be more industrial`
- `C too dark for trust`
- `C composition is strongest`
- `Keep A structure with B warmth`

## What To Review

### 1. Heading Attitude

React to size, confidence, and tone.

- `too big`
- `too small`
- `too aggressive`
- `not confident enough`
- `best hierarchy`

### 2. Spacing And Density

React to how tight or loose the board feels.

- `too tight`
- `too open`
- `good rhythm`
- `feels cramped`
- `feels calm`

### 3. Color Balance

React to the mood and ratio of the palette, not just whether you like one color in isolation.

- `too dark`
- `too beige`
- `accent too loud`
- `palette feels premium`
- `needs more contrast`

### 4. Imagery Direction

React to whether the imagery feels credible, supportive, and appropriate for the company.

- `images too dominant`
- `images too generic`
- `good proof energy`
- `feels more architecture than construction`
- `too cinematic`

### 5. Surface Treatment

React to corners, borders, textures, and overall polish level.

- `too boxy`
- `too flat`
- `too decorative`
- `corners too soft`
- `surface treatment works`

### 6. Composition

React to hierarchy, focal structure, and distinctiveness.

- `looks too much like a webpage`
- `strong board composition`
- `too template-like`
- `best focal move`
- `not distinct enough`

## Concept-Specific Watchouts

### Concept A: Measured Steel

- Best quality to preserve: operational discipline
- Watch for: becoming too harsh, too software-like, or too orange

### Concept B: Civic Craft

- Best quality to preserve: calm trust and maturity
- Watch for: becoming too soft, too architectural, or too real-estate-like

### Concept C: Night Shift Precision

- Best quality to preserve: command and technical sharpness
- Watch for: becoming too dark, too sci-fi, or too intimidating

## Decision Prompt

Reply in any of these formats:

- `Approve A`
- `Approve B`
- `Approve C`
- `A with warmer palette`
- `B with more construction proof`
- `C but less dark`
- `None yet, A and C still too similar in tone`

## Reminder

Judge these boards for visual direction only:

- typography
- color
- spacing
- imagery
- composition
- surface treatment

Do not use the generated wording inside the images as final copy guidance.
