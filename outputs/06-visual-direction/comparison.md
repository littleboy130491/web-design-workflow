# Visual Direction Comparison

## Overview

These three directions were developed from the strategy brief and audience-positioning outputs for `PT Konstruksi Pratama Nusantara`. All three aim to make the company feel credible, organized, and capable for commercial and industrial work, but they signal trust in different ways.

## Concept Snapshot

### Concept A: Measured Steel

- Feels disciplined, operational, and proof-first
- Uses a rigid vertical composition, industrial neutrals, and a controlled safety-orange accent
- Best when the company should feel structured, accountable, and easy to evaluate

### Concept B: Civic Craft

- Feels warmer, calmer, and more editorial
- Uses a softer mineral palette, serif-led hierarchy, and daylight project imagery
- Best when the company should feel mature, reassuring, and easy to trust quickly

### Concept C: Night Shift Precision

- Feels assertive, technical, and command-oriented
- Uses a dark high-contrast palette, banded composition, and stronger cinematic energy
- Best when the company should feel sharp, decisive, and capable of handling complex delivery conditions

## Key Differences

### Typography

- `Concept A` uses compact technical type energy and feels the most operational.
- `Concept B` uses editorial serif contrast and feels the most mature and human.
- `Concept C` uses oversized forceful type and feels the most commanding.

### Color

- `Concept A` balances graphite and limestone with a restrained orange signal.
- `Concept B` is the warmest palette and feels the least industrial.
- `Concept C` creates the strongest mood through the dark background and amber signal.

### Composition

- `Concept A` has the clearest specimen-board discipline.
- `Concept B` has the calmest asymmetry and the most breathing room.
- `Concept C` has the strongest banded hierarchy and the most dramatic rhythm.

### Imagery

- `Concept A` uses site proof most directly.
- `Concept B` makes imagery feel curated and reassuring.
- `Concept C` makes imagery feel forceful and high-pressure.

## Visible Risks In The Current Images

- `Concept A` still shows generated text substitutions, so it should be judged on hierarchy, palette, and structure rather than literal wording.
- `Concept B` risks drifting toward real-estate or architectural-brand language if the final implementation loses enough construction proof.
- `Concept C` risks feeling too severe or too dark for first-contact trust if the later site does not soften it with enough clarity and daylight imagery.

## Recommendation

### Primary Recommendation: Concept A

`Concept A` is the strongest fit for the current strategy. It expresses operational maturity, delivery discipline, and proof-first credibility without becoming generic corporate services design or over-dramatic dark branding.

### Secondary Recommendation: Concept B

`Concept B` is the best alternative if the company should feel more reassuring and mature, especially for business owners who may respond better to warmth than hard-edged technical signaling.

### Select Concept C If

Choose `Concept C` only if the brand should deliberately lead with control, urgency, and technical command, and if a darker visual system is acceptable for first impressions.

## Suggested Approval Lens

When choosing, focus on these questions:

- Which concept makes the company feel like the safer choice rather than the cheaper choice?
- Which concept would help a developer or business owner trust the company within the first 30 seconds?
- Which concept feels distinctive without becoming theatrical or template-like?
